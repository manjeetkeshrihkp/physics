Ray Optics & Optical Instruments

Physics • Class 12 • Chapter 9

Detailed Physics Notes

Comprehensive notes based on NCERT Class 12 Physics - Chapter 9

1. Reflection of Light

Light travels in straight lines (rectilinear propagation). When it encounters a polished surface, it bounces back.

Laws of Reflection

  • Angle of incidence (\(i\)) equals angle of reflection (\(r\)).
  • The incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in the same plane.

Spherical Mirrors

Concave Mirror: Converges parallel rays. Convex Mirror: Diverges parallel rays.

Focal Length (\(f\)): The distance from the pole to the focus. For spherical mirrors with radius \(R\), \(f = R/2\).

Figure 9.1 (Animated): A ray parallel to the principal axis reflects and passes through the Focus (F).

Mirror Equation:

$$ \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$

Magnification (\(m\)): \(m = \frac{h'}{h} = -\frac{v}{u}\).

2. Refraction of Light

Bending of light when passing from one transparent medium to another.

Snell's Law

$$ n_1 \sin i = n_2 \sin r \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = n_{21} $$

Where \(n_{21} = n_2/n_1\) is the refractive index of medium 2 w.r.t medium 1.

Apparent Depth

An object in a denser medium appears raised when viewed from a rarer medium. $$ \text{Apparent Depth} = \frac{\text{Real Depth}}{n} $$

3. Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

When light travels from denser to rarer medium, if angle of incidence (\(i\)) > critical angle (\(i_c\)), the ray reflects back into the denser medium.

$$ \sin i_c = \frac{1}{n} $$
Figure 9.2 (Animated): Light guiding in an Optical Fiber via successive Total Internal Reflections.

Applications:

  • Mirage: Optical illusion in deserts/hot roads due to varying air density.
  • Diamond: Brilliance is due to TIR (\(i_c \approx 24.4^\circ\)).
  • Prisms: Used to bend light by \(90^\circ\) or \(180^\circ\).
  • Optical Fibers: Transmit signals with negligible loss.

4. Refraction at Spherical Surfaces

Formula for refraction at a single spherical surface:

$$ \frac{n_2}{v} - \frac{n_1}{u} = \frac{n_2 - n_1}{R} $$

5. Lenses

A lens creates an image by refraction.

Figure 9.3 (Animated): A convex lens converges parallel rays to the focal point.

Lens Maker's Formula

$$ \frac{1}{f} = (n - 1) \left( \frac{1}{R_1} - \frac{1}{R_2} \right) $$

Thin Lens Formula

$$ \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$

Power of a Lens (\(P\))

Reciprocal of focal length (in meters). Unit: Diopter (D). $$ P = \frac{1}{f} $$

Combination of Lenses: \(P = P_1 + P_2 + \dots\)

6. Refraction through a Prism

Light bends towards the base of a prism. The angle between incident and emergent rays is the Angle of Deviation (\(\delta\)).

$$ \delta = i + e - A $$

At Minimum Deviation (\(\delta_m\)), \(i=e\) and \(r_1=r_2\). Refractive index is:

$$ n = \frac{\sin \frac{A + \delta_m}{2}}{\sin \frac{A}{2}} $$
Figure 9.4 (Animated): Dispersion of white light into its constituent spectrum.

Dispersion: Splitting of white light into colors. Violet bends the most, Red the least.

Rainbow: Caused by dispersion, refraction, and internal reflection in rain droplets.

7. Optical Instruments

Simple Microscope

A single convex lens acting as a magnifier. Magnifying Power (\(m\)):

  • Image at Near Point (\(D\)): \(m = 1 + \frac{D}{f}\)
  • Image at Infinity: \(m = \frac{D}{f}\)

Compound Microscope

Two lenses: Objective (small \(f_o\)) and Eyepiece (moderate \(f_e\)).

$$ m = m_o \times m_e \approx \frac{L}{f_o} \left( 1 + \frac{D}{f_e} \right) $$

Astronomical Telescope

Objective (large \(f_o\), large aperture) and Eyepiece (small \(f_e\)).

$$ m = \frac{f_o}{f_e} $$

Reflecting Telescope (Cassegrain): Uses a parabolic mirror as objective to eliminate chromatic aberration and reduce weight.
